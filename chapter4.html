The Coincidence I Couldn't Escape

Next semester… you weren’t in my class anymore.

Not in my section. Not in my routine. Not in my everyday view.

And slowly, life became normal again.

No more waiting for the door to open. No more pretending not to look. No more racing heartbeat.

Time did what it usually does.

It faded things.

I won’t lie. I thought maybe it was just a phase. Maybe it was just proximity. Maybe it was just imagination.

And without seeing you every day, the intensity became softer.

I told myself, “See? It wasn’t that deep.”

And then… summer term happened.

And somehow, out of all the people, out of all the timings, out of all the coincidences…

There you were again.

Standing there. Existing like you always do.

And just like that, every single flashback hit me.

The late entries. The quiet exits. The stare after I said something stupid. The day you sat too close.

All of it came back at once.

And this time, it didn’t feel small.

It felt real.

And then life added another twist.

We had a mutual — Arun.

I remember thinking, “Of course. Why wouldn’t the universe do this?”

The more I tried to ignore you, the more you appeared.

Same campus. Same air. Same unexpected encounters.

I rarely saw you in classes during that time. It wasn’t like before.

But whenever I did see you…

I smiled.

Not a big smile. Not something obvious.

Just a small one. The kind that happens automatically.

And I remember asking myself — “What is this behavior?”

Why does my heart react like this? Why does one person keep reappearing every time I try to move on?

Was it coincidence?

Or was it something quietly pulling us back into each other’s orbit?

I don’t know.

But I do know this —

That summer, I stopped pretending it was nothing.

Because emotions don’t return that strongly if they were never real.

And every time I saw you again, it felt less like accident… and more like something that was meant to happen.
