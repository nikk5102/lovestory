The Day You Sat Too Close

You know one thing about me?

I always sit in the same place.

Same corner. Same bench. Same side of the classroom.

I never change my seat. Not for anyone.

It’s my little safe space.

But that day…

You came and sat on the next corner.

So close.

Not across the room. Not a few benches away.

Just there. One small distance between us.

And I don’t think you will ever understand what that did to me.

My heartbeat became so loud I was scared someone else could hear it.

I tried to look normal. I tried to focus on the lecture. I tried to act like it was just another day.

But it wasn’t.

Because you were too close.

Close enough for me to notice the way you held your mobile. The way you leaned back. The way you slightly turned.

And the more I tried to ignore you, the more my eyes betrayed me.

I couldn’t focus. Not on the teacher. Not on the board. Not even on my own notebook.

My mind was blank. My heart was racing.

And I couldn’t tell anyone.

I couldn’t tell my friends, “Why is he sitting so close?”

I couldn’t say, “I can’t breathe properly right now.”

So I did the only thing I could think of.

For the very first time… I changed my seat.

Me. The girl who never moves.

I switched seats with my friend.

Because you were too close to me.

And my eyes… my heartbeat… my thoughts…

None of them were in my control anymore.

I was scared that if I stayed there, you would somehow hear everything my heart was screaming.

And maybe you didn’t even notice.

Maybe for you, it was just another seat. Another normal day.

But for me?

It was the first time I realized this wasn’t just a small crush.

Because if someone’s presence can shake your focus like that…

Then it’s not something simple.

Even now, when I think about that day, I can feel that same fast heartbeat.

And I smile.

Because you had no idea…

That just by sitting beside me, you had already taken control of something I thought I was protecting.
