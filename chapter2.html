The Day I Said Something Stupid

I still remember the exact day I found out your name.

You were sitting on the next bench to ours. Not far. Just close enough for me to notice everything.

My friend casually said your name. Just like that. So normal.

And before I could even think… before I could control my mouth… I replied automatically —

“I had a bad experience with this name. I stay away from people with this name.”

I don’t know why I said it like that. Maybe it was reflex. Maybe it was fear. Maybe it was me trying to protect myself before anything even began.

And then…

You just stared at me.

Not angry. Not smiling. Just… looking.

And in that moment, I don’t know what you thought about me.

Maybe you thought I was rude. Maybe you thought I had attitude. Maybe you thought, “What kind of girl says something like that?”

And honestly?

I wanted the ground to swallow me.

Because the truth was — I didn’t hate your name.

I was scared of it.

I was scared of feeling something again.

And do you know something else?

When my friend told me you were from Alwar… my heart paused.

Because I am from Alwar too.

But I never told you.

I let you believe I was just from Jodhpur.

I hid that tiny connection on purpose.

Because if we were from the same place, if we had something in common, it would become harder to ignore you.

And I was trying so hard to end whatever this feeling was before it even started.

So I built distance. Fake distance.

But the funny thing is…

Even after saying I stay away from that name, I never stayed away from you.

My eyes still searched for you every time the door opened.

I still noticed when you were late. I still noticed when you left early.

And now, while writing this…

I can still see your face from that day.

The way you looked at me.

And I’m smiling.

Because if I could go back, I would say something different.

I would say — “I’m not scared of your name. I’m scared of how easily you’re becoming important.”

But maybe… if I hadn’t said something stupid that day, we wouldn’t be here now.

And maybe… you wouldn’t be reading this.
