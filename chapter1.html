The Boy Who Was Always Late

The first time I noticed you… you weren’t even sitting in class.

You entered late.

The door opened softly, and for a second everyone looked up — including me.

You walked in like it wasn’t a big deal. Calm. Unbothered and smiling.. Like time didn’t control you.

And I don’t know why… but that was the exact moment my eyes stayed on you a little longer than they should have.

It wasn’t love. It wasn’t even a crush yet.

It was curiosity.

And I don’t know why… but something inside me shifted.

You just came in… sat down… and existed.

And somehow that felt different.

After that day, I started noticing a pattern.

You were always a little late.

And every time the door opened during lecture, my heart would do something small and stupid.

“Is it him?”

Sometimes it was. Sometimes it wasn’t.

But on the days it was…

I would pretend to look at my notebook while secretly watching you walk to your seat.

And then I started thinking something else.

I thought you must be very loyal to someone.

I used to imagine that maybe there was a girl waiting for you somewhere… someone you were completely committed to.

Because the way you never looked at any girl — not even once properly — shocked me.

You never tried to impress anyone. Never flirted. Never even let your eyes wander.

And I used to sit there wondering… what kind of person is he?

Does he not find anyone interesting? Or is he already someone’s?

I convinced myself that maybe you couldn’t even stay a second without her.

That maybe the moment class gave you freedom… you were rushing to go see her.

Whenever you got the smallest chance — attendance done, lecture boring, teacher distracted —

you would quietly leave.

Just gone.

And I don’t think you’ll ever understand this…

But every time you stood up to leave, my eyes followed you till the door closed.

And when it did?

The class suddenly felt… a little emptier.

It’s funny, isn’t it?

We never talk.

Not even once properly.

But my day somehow depended on whether you showed up late or disappeared early.

You never knew.

You never knew that someone sitting a few benches away had memorized your timing.

That someone’s eyes searched for the door every single lecture.

That someone noticed when you weren’t there.

And maybe it was silly.

Maybe it was just a quiet girl finding something beautiful in the most ordinary boy.

But for me…

You weren’t just the boy who came late.

You were the reason I started looking at the door more than the board.
