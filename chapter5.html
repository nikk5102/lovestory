talk Chapter 5 — The First Time We

The First Time We talk

And then came fourth year.

Guess what?

We were in the same class again.

Are you seeing the coincidence now?

After semesters of distance, after fading feelings, after pretending it was nothing…

There you were again. Same classroom. Same air. Same presence.

But something had changed in me.

I wasn’t panicking anymore. I wasn’t switching seats.

I had gotten used to it.

When I saw you, I smiled.

And when you saw me smiling, you smiled back.

That’s it.

No long conversations. No dramatic moments.

Just silent smiles.

But you know what?

My smile always came first.

I would see you… and without even deciding, my lips would curve.

And then you would notice. And then you would smile back.

It became our tiny untalkn thing.

And then one day…

You came and sat beside me.

Just like that.

Not for long. Just for a few minutes.

But those few minutes?

My heart remembered everything.

The old days. The fast heartbeat. The seat I once changed because of you.

And then you said something.

So casual. So normal.

“Mark me present.”

Proxy.

And guess what?

I couldn’t even deny.

I didn’t argue. I didn’t hesitate.

I just said, “Okay”

And that…

That was the first time I ever talk to you.

Not a confession. Not a deep conversation.

Just one word.

“Present.”

Funny, right?

The first word I ever said to you was making sure you were marked as present…

When you had already been present in my mind for years.

And then, like you always used to do…

You left the class.

Calm. Casual. Like it was nothing.

And just like that…

Our college days ended.

No big goodbye. No dramatic closure.

Just a few smiles, one proxy, and a thousand untalkn feelings.

If someone had told me that day that one day you would be reading this…

I would have laughed.

Because back then, we were just two people sharing a classroom.

And now…

You’re so much more than that.
